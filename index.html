Thus far, we've been dealing with a single page - our index.html - and it's time to branch out.

Anyone who ever owned a Myspace back in it's heyday may be very familiar with the following HTML:

<a href="...">

<img src="...">


Let's take a closer look at each of these, starting with <a href="...">

Here, the a refers to the <a> tag of the anchor element - one of the most fundamental and widely used tags in HTML. Its primary purpose is to create hyperlinks, which are connections from one resource to another.

The href or hyperlink reference that follows is an attribute of the anchor element.

You can probably guess that the content inside of the ="..." is the attribute's value, which specifies the destination URL of the hyperlink reference created by the <a> element.


So.. putting this all together:

  • The <a> element marks its content as clickable. Like this: I am clickable text!
  • The href attribute establishes that the clickable content is hyperlinked - that it can be interacted with to navigate to another resource.
  • And the value entered between the quotation marks in ="..." is the destination that the hyperlink navigates to!
